    Finanical Aid and Scholarships

    All students are encouraged to apply for financial assistance to meet the costs of attending the university.
    The two categories of financial aid are "Need-Based Financial Aid and Merit Scholarships


    Need-Based Financial Aid

    Students seeking admissio to the univesity are required to complete the request for financial
    finanical aid on the aapplication form. The need for financial aid is determined by subtracting the resources
    available to a student from the total cost fo attending the university. Once admitted into the university
    students are required to register for at least 18 credit hours per semester, maintain a GPA of at least 3.0, and not be on academic or disciplinary probation


    Benefit

    1. Tuition Assistance
    2. Work study opportunities
    3. Free access to university and partners libraries

    Merit Scholarships

    Scholarships are availabel, without a determination of need, to students who meet criteria stipulated
    by donors (GPA) usually 3.0 or better, pursuing a specific course of study, and/or having residence in a certain
    geographic location. A complete listing of these scholarships is available from the Office of Financial Aid.


    Deadlines

    New students: Semester I March 15
    Semester II September 15

    Old students: 3 weeks before registration begins
